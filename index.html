How this works

Libraries we know exist but haven't been scanned yet — still waiting for their moment
Libraries with at least one inventory on record
To find libraries near you
Tap 📍 at the top of the screen to centre the map on your location.
To see what's on a library's shelf
Tap its pin to open it, then look at the inventory section. You'll see the most recent shelf photos and the book titles read from them.
To search all libraries
Tap 🔍 at the top of the screen. Search by title or author to find every library where that book's been spotted recently, or switch to the Genre tab to browse libraries by what they're stocked with — both sorted by distance from you, so you're not driving to three libraries hoping one has it.
To add an inventory
Tap a pin to open the library. No inventory yet? Tap "📷 Scan the shelf" and take a photo. We'll read the spines automatically — you can fix anything we got wrong before saving. For best results, step back to get the whole interior in frame — one photo of both shelves beats two close-ups. Can't fit it all in one shot? After saving, we'll ask if you need a second picture to complete the inventory.
To add or update a photo of the library box
Open a library and tap 🏡 Update box photo at the bottom of the screen.
To add a new library
Tap ＋ at the top of the screen.
To see your stats
Tap 📊 at the top of the screen to see how many libraries you've scanned, how many books you've helped catalogue, and how many friends you've brought along — plus badges earned for helping make Little Library Librarian more searchable. All counted from scans made on your own device — no account needed.

About this project

We're Jill and Joel, a writer and a professor, who have always loved little free libraries. We walk to them daily — usually with our dogs — sometimes hunting for the next book in a series we started from a box three streets over, sometimes just curious what the neighbourhood left behind this week.
Then one day we were looking for a specific book. The bookstore didn't have it. The library didn't have it either. So we started checking little free libraries around us, each time coming home empty-handed.
So Jill wondered out loud: what if you could search little libraries by title or genre — like checking gas prices? Joel loved the idea.
And now you're looking at it.
The more libraries people log, the more useful it gets for everyone — including you. Every scan you add helps someone find their next great read, and their scans help you find yours.
If you're reading this, you're a friend or a friend of a friend. We need about 500 libraries logged to get this moving on its own.
So if you're in: log a library today. Save it to your home screen. Scan a shelf. Then share it with three people who love to walk and read.
Thanks for being here (before it gets cool!).
We appreciate your help cataloguing our community's book shelves!

— Jill and Joel

Where the map came from
This started with location data from the Greater Victoria Placemaking Network, who've spent years mapping little libraries across the region — our thanks to them for the head start. Everything since then, every inventory and box photo, comes from people like you.
Your privacy
No account, no sign-up, no personal information. Your device gets an anonymous ID so we can show you your own stats — that's it.
